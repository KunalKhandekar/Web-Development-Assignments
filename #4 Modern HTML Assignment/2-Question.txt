2. What are HTML Entities? List out 5 commonly used HTML entities ?

Answer:

HTML entities are special codes or character references used to represent characters that have a specific meaning or are not directly typable on a keyboard. They are often used to display reserved characters, symbols, or special characters within HTML documents.

Some are as follow:

    1. &amp; - Represents the ampersand symbol "&".
    
    2. &lt; - Represents the less-than symbol "<".
    
    3. &gt; - Represents the greater-than symbol ">".
    
    4. &quot; - Represents the quotation mark character "".
    
    5. &nbsp; - Represents a non-breaking space.
